# Paper Brief

## Target Journal

Primary: Journal of Product Innovation Management  
Secondary: Journal of Marketing Research / Marketing Science

## Paper Type

Empirical cultural product innovation / novelty and market response paper

## Working Title

Genre Atypicality and Market Response in Cultural Products: Evidence from Books and Films

## Research Question

How does a cultural product's atypicality relative to its genre affect market response?

## Core Phenomenon

Cultural products such as books and films are evaluated within genre systems. Some products closely follow the language, themes, or keywords that are typical of their genre, while others deviate from genre norms. This project studies whether such genre atypicality affects two forms of market response: popularity and evaluation.

## Theoretical Motivation

The project is motivated by the tension between familiarity and novelty. Products that are highly typical may be easier for consumers to understand and categorize, but they may also lack distinctiveness. Products that are highly atypical may stand out and signal novelty, but they may also create uncertainty, reduce fit with consumer expectations, or make evaluation more difficult.

## JPIM Relevance

The project is relevant to Journal of Product Innovation Management because it studies how novelty, atypicality, and category fit shape market response to cultural products. Cultural products are innovation-intensive offerings whose success depends on balancing distinctiveness with recognizability. The paper can contribute to innovation management by showing how genre-based atypicality is associated with product popularity and evaluation.

## Empirical Setting

The main empirical setting is Goodreads books.

The supplementary empirical setting is TMDb movies.

The Goodreads analysis uses book-level metadata, including:

- book title
- star rating
- number of ratings
- number of reviews
- summary text
- genre information
- first published year

The TMDb analysis uses movie-level metadata, including:

- movie title
- overview
- genres
- keywords
- popularity
- vote average
- vote count
- release date

## Key Construct

The key independent variable is product atypicality.

For Goodreads, atypicality is measured using words from book summaries relative to typical words in the book's classified genre.

For TMDb, atypicality is measured using movie keywords relative to typical keywords in the movie's primary genre.

## Main Dependent Variables

The project studies two types of market response:

1. Popularity
   - Goodreads: log number of ratings
   - TMDb: log number of votes / vote count based popularity measure

2. Evaluation
   - Goodreads: star rating
   - TMDb: vote average

## Main Empirical Idea

The paper estimates whether genre atypicality predicts product popularity and product evaluation after accounting for summary length, genre fixed effects, and release decade fixed effects.

## Main Hypotheses / Empirical Questions

### H1: Main effect of atypicality

Genre atypicality is associated with product-market response.

The direction may differ depending on whether the outcome is popularity or evaluation.

### H1-U: Nonlinear effect of atypicality

The relationship between genre atypicality and market response may be nonlinear. This tests whether moderate atypicality performs differently from very low or very high atypicality.

### H2: Typical versus atypical products

Products classified as typical may differ from products classified as atypical in popularity and evaluation.

### H2-3: Typical, moderate, and radical products

Market response may differ across three levels of atypicality: typical, moderate, and radical.

### H3: Genre context moderation

The effect of atypicality may depend on genre-level context, including the mean level of atypicality within the genre and the dispersion of atypicality within the genre.

## Core Contribution

The paper contributes by developing a text-based and genre-relative measurement approach for cultural product atypicality and applying it to large-scale market response data.

The project contributes to research on:

- product innovation and cultural product novelty
- category typicality and atypicality
- genre-based product positioning
- novelty-familiarity trade-offs
- popularity versus evaluation in cultural markets
- computational measurement of product positioning using text and metadata

## Important Caution

The empirical design is observational. The manuscript should avoid claiming randomized causal effects unless stronger identification strategies are added.

The results should be interpreted as evidence of conditional associations between genre atypicality and market response.

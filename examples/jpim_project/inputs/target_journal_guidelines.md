# Target Journal Guidelines

## Primary Target

Journal of Product Innovation Management

## Secondary Targets

Journal of Marketing Research  
Marketing Science

## JPIM Writing Priorities

The manuscript should emphasize:

- product innovation relevance
- cultural product novelty
- genre atypicality
- novelty-familiarity trade-off
- product positioning
- market response to innovation
- managerial relevance for cultural product creators, publishers, producers, and platform managers

## JPIM Contribution Requirements

The paper should clearly explain:

- what product innovation phenomenon is being studied
- why cultural products are an appropriate innovation context
- how genre atypicality captures novelty relative to category expectations
- how atypicality relates to popularity and evaluation
- why popularity and evaluation are distinct market outcomes
- how the paper advances theory on novelty, category fit, and product-market response
- what managers should do differently when designing, positioning, or promoting cultural products

## Empirical Writing Priorities

The manuscript should be transparent about:

- data source
- sample construction
- genre classification
- text and keyword preprocessing
- atypicality measurement
- dependent variables
- controls
- fixed effects
- robust standard errors
- observational design limitations

## Style Rules

- Avoid generic statements about innovation.
- Avoid generic statements about AI or machine learning.
- Do not present computational measures as the main contribution by themselves.
- Make the theoretical contribution about product atypicality and market response.
- Distinguish popularity from evaluation.
- Avoid causal claims unless the empirical design supports them.
- Use precise language such as "associated with" or "predicts" when discussing observational results.
- Explain managerial implications for publishers, producers, authors, filmmakers, and platform managers.
- Tie every managerial implication back to the novelty-familiarity trade-off.

## Suggested Positioning

The manuscript should be positioned as a study of cultural product innovation.

The core argument should be:

Cultural products must balance genre fit and novelty. By measuring how atypical a product is relative to its genre, the paper examines whether deviations from genre norms help or hurt market response.

## Suggested Contribution Statement

The paper contributes to product innovation research by showing how genre-relative atypicality, measured from product descriptions and keywords, is associated with cultural product popularity and evaluation. It extends research on novelty and category fit by distinguishing between market attention and consumer evaluation, and by testing whether atypicality has linear, nonlinear, and context-dependent relationships with market response.

## Claims to Avoid

Avoid claiming:

- atypicality causes success
- radical products always perform better
- typical products always perform better
- text-based atypicality fully captures innovation
- Goodreads and TMDb represent all cultural product markets

## Preferred Claim Language

Use:

- "genre atypicality is associated with..."
- "the findings are consistent with..."
- "the evidence suggests..."
- "the pattern indicates..."
- "conditional on genre and release-period controls..."
